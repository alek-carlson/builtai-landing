Questions

Good questions.

What can the assistant actually do?

Summarize documents, extract action items, draft content, organize files, manage calendar, triage email, run scheduled tasks, and execute workflows you define. Integrates with Google Workspace, Notion, GitHub, and more.

Is this safe? Can it access my bank accounts?

It only has access to what you explicitly connect. Most people connect productivity tools — not banking. The system has approval gates for destructive actions, and you stay in control.

Why a Mac Mini instead of a cloud server?

Residential IPs get blocked less than cloud IPs — fewer CAPTCHAs, more services just work. Mac-only integrations like iMessage, Apple Notes, and Reminders are built in. And you own the hardware — no recurring compute costs.

Do I need to be technical?

No. You interact via chat in natural language — Discord, Telegram, iMessage, or WhatsApp. Technical setup is done for you.

What's the difference between Ship It, Remote, and In-Person?

Ship It ($2,499) is the flagship — I source a Mac Mini, configure it completely, and ship it ready to use. Plug in, power on, start delegating. Remote Setup ($999) is for folks who already have a Mac Mini or spare MacBook — we hop on a call and I configure it remotely. In-Person ($1,499 + hardware at cost) is white glove service for the LA/Long Beach area — I come to you, set everything up in your environment, and make sure it's working before I leave.

Why not set this up myself?

You could. Most people spend 10-30 hours researching and configuring, debugging permissions, and still end up with something fragile. About 70% abandon the project. With BuiltAI, you get a known-good architecture, tested workflows, safe defaults, and a working system on day one.

What AI models does it use?

You're not locked to any provider. Most use Claude for complex tasks, cheaper models for simple stuff. Switch models with one command.

What about updates and maintenance?

14 days of hypercare included — I'm on call to help you dial in your setup. After that, optional care plans cover ongoing updates and tweaks. Or maintain it yourself — it's your machine.
